CUTTING-DIET

aaa

A cutting diet is focused on reducing body fat while preserving muscle, commonly followed by bodybuilders and fitness enthusiasts during a "cutting phase." The key is to create a calorie deficit by eating fewer calories than the body requires, while maintaining a high protein intake to protect muscle mass.

Key Principles of a Cutting Diet:

  • High Protein: Aim for lean protein sources to preserve muscle mass.
  • Low to Moderate Carbs: Focus on slow-digesting carbs like whole grains, and reduce overall carb intake, especially refined carbs and sugars.
  • Healthy Fats: Incorporate fats from good sources like nuts, seeds, and ghee in moderation.
  • Hydration: Drink plenty of water throughout the day.
  • Meal Timing: Eat smaller, frequent meals throughout the day to keep metabolism active.

Starches:

Old-fashioned oats • Pre-rinsed quinoa • Basmati rice • Brown rice • Sweet potatoes • Russet potatoes • Chickpeas • Pumpkin puree (not pie filling) • Black beans

Proteins:

Large eggs • Liquid egg whites • Greek yogurt • 1% cottage cheese • Whole chicken • 99% lean ground turkey • Medium shrimp • Lean ground beef • Tilapia fillets • Cod fillets • Pork tenderloin • Canned tuna • Boneless skinless chicken breasts

Fruits and Vegetables:

Navel oranges • White onions • Broccoli • Fresh baby spinach • Chopped kale • Frozen riced cauliflower • Fresh cauliflower • Cabbage • Acorn squash • Watermelon • Cantaloupe • Avocados • Apples • Zucchini • Yellow squash • Beefsteak tomatoes

Other Items:

Coconut oil • Whey protein powder • Powdered stevia • Unsweetened almond milk • Almond flour • Powdered peanut butter • Low-sodium chicken broth • Natural almond butter • Unsweetened cocoa powder • Jarred minced garlic

SUGGESTED RECIPES

Breakfast

  • Egg whites omelette (with spinach, tomatoes, onions) cooked in minimal olive oil.
  • 1 slice of whole wheat bread or multigrain roti.
  • Black coffee or green tea (no sugar).
  • Scrambled eggs (2 whole eggs + 3 egg whites).
  • 1 bowl of oats with almond milk, a few nuts, and seeds.

Lunch

  • Grilled/boiled chicken breast or grilled fish.
  • A large portion of salad (cucumber, broccoli, tomatoes, lettuce, onions, and olive oil dressing).
  • 1-2 rotis (multigrain).
  • Tandoori chicken (skinless, marinated in curd and spices, baked or grilled).
  • Sautéed vegetables (spinach, bell peppers, broccoli) with garlic.
  • Brown rice (small portion).
  • Chicken curry (made with minimal oil, without cream).
  • Brown rice or quinoa (small portion).

    DINNER

  • Grilled fish (salmon, tilapia, or rohu) with a side of stir-fried vegetables (broccoli, beans, carrots).
  • Clear chicken soup or lentil soup.
  • Chicken tikka (grilled) or baked chicken with Indian spices.
  • Vegetable stir fry (use minimal oil, with garlic, cumin, and coriander for flavor).
  • Whole wheat chapathi (1-2).
  • Egg curry (made with minimal oil, boiled eggs).
  • Sautéed spinach or cabbage with mustard seeds and a bit of turmeric.
  • Whole wheat chapathi (1-2).

Snacks

  • Greek yogurt with berries and nuts.
  • Protein bar or protein shake.
  • Vegetable sticks (carrots, celery) with hummus.
  • Roasted chickpeas or mixed nuts (handful).
  • Fruits (apple, orange, banana).

    Before Bed

  • 1 glass of warm milk (low fat) or almond milk with a pinch of turmeric (optional).
  • Handful of almonds or a spoonful of peanut butter for healthy fats.

ADDITIONAL TIPS

  • Spices: Indian spices like turmeric, cumin, coriander, ginger, and garlic are not only flavorful but also help in boosting metabolism.
  • Protein: Aim for 1.6-2g of protein per kg of body weight.
  • Carbs: Prefer complex carbs like oats, quinoa, brown rice, and millets (ragi, bajra).
  • Healthy Fats: Include sources like almonds, walnuts, flax seeds, and olive oil in moderation.
  • Cooking Methods: Opt for grilling, baking, steaming, and stir-frying with minimal oil.
