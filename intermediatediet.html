INTERMITTENT FASTING

PREMIUM

Intermittent fasting (IF) can be effectively combined with a muscle-building diet. The key is to ensure you consume enough calories and macronutrients during the eating window to support muscle growth and recovery

Key Principles for Muscle Building with IF:

    1. Determine Your Fasting Window:
      • Common IF protocols: 16:8 (16 hours fasting, 8 hours eating) or 18:6.
      • Adjust the eating window to fit your schedule, e.g., 12 PM–8 PM or 1 PM–7 PM.
    2. Prioritize Calorie and Protein Intake:
      • Ensure you consume sufficient calories during the eating window to support muscle growth.
      • Aim for 1.6–2.2g of protein per kg of body weight.
    3. Meal Timing Within the Eating Window:
      • Break Fast: Start with a balanced, nutrient-dense meal to replenish energy.
      • Pre-Workout Meal: Include carbs and protein for energy and performance.
      • Post-Workout Meal: Prioritize protein and carbs to support recovery.
    4. Macros for Muscle Building:
      • Protein: Chicken, eggs, fish, tofu, paneer, lentils.
      • Carbs: Brown rice, quinoa, sweet potatoes, oats, fruits.
      • Fats: Nuts, seeds, ghee, avocado, fatty fish.
      • Veggies: Include fiber-rich, nutrient-dense vegetables.
    5. Hydration:
      • Drink water during the fasting period to stay hydrated.
      • Black coffee, green tea, and herbal teas are allowed.

SUGGESTED RECIPES

Breakfast( 12:00 PM – 8:00 PM)

  • Grilled chicken breast with a bowl of quinoa, sautéed spinach, and a side of avocado.
  • 3 boiled eggs with multigrain toast, a handful of roasted almonds, and a green smoothie.
  • Tofu or paneer stir-fry with brown rice and cucumber salad.

Pre-Workout Meal(2:30 PM)

  • Sweet potato chaat with a boiled egg or grilled fish.
  • A banana with 1 tbsp peanut butter and 1 boiled egg.
  • Greek yogurt with honey, granola, and mixed berries.

    Post-Workout Meal(4:30 PM)

  • Protein shake with almond milk and a handful of dates or figs.
  • Grilled chicken or prawns with a small portion of millet roti and sautéed broccoli.
  • Omelet with mixed vegetables and a slice of whole-grain bread.

Dinner(7:00 PM)

  • Tandoori fish or chicken tikka with stir-fried vegetables.
  • Egg curry with brown rice or a millet roti and a side of curd.
  • Mutton curry (lean cuts) with a small serving of quinoa and green salad.

    Snack(Before 8 PM)(Optional)

  • A handful of mixed nuts (almonds, walnuts, pistachios).
  • A glass of turmeric milk with a pinch of cinnamon.
  • A boiled egg or a piece of dark chocolate.

ADDITIONAL TIPS

  • 1. Morning Workouts (During Fasting):
    • If working out fasted, ensure your first meal is nutrient-dense and includes carbs, protein, and fats.
    • Consider BCAAs or a light pre-workout (if needed).
  • 2. Midday/Evening Workouts:
    • Consume a pre-workout meal 1–2 hours before and a high-protein post-workout meal within an hour after.
