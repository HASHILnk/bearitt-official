WOMEN'S-DIET PLAN

PREMIUM AVAILABLE

To maintain a good body structure and overall health, women can focus on a combination of balanced nutrition, exercise, and healthy habits.

Customizing for Your Needs

For Bulking: Add an extra serving of carbs (e.g., rice or roti) and increase the portion size of protein-rich foods like paneer or tofu.

For Maintenance/Lean Muscle: Keep portions moderate and focus on whole grains and fiber-rich vegetables.

Key Principles for Nutrition

  • 1. Protein: Essential for muscle repair and growth.
    Sources: Eggs, chicken, fish, turkey, lean mutton, prawns.
  • 2. Carbohydrates: For energy and recovery.
    Sources: Whole grains (brown rice, quinoa, roti), sweet potatoes, oats, fruits.
  • 3. Healthy Fats: For hormonal health and energy.
    Sources: Nuts, seeds, ghee, coconut, avocado.
  • 4. Vegetables: For vitamins, minerals, and fiber.
    Include greens, root vegetables, and colorful options.
  • 5. Hydration: Drink plenty of water and include electrolyte-rich drinks like coconut water.

ADDITIONAL TIPS

  • Supplements: Consider supplementing with:
    • Whey protein (to meet protein goals)
    • Creatine (to support strength and muscle growth)
    • BCAA (for recovery)
    • Fish oil (for healthy fats and joint support)
  • Caloric Tracking: Track your calories if possible, to ensure you're in a surplus without gaining unnecessary fat.
  • Cooking Methods: Opt for grilling, baking, steaming, and stir-frying with minimal oil.

Cutting Diet Plan for Women

General Guidelines:
• Meal Frequency: 3 main meals + 2 light snacks.
• Cooking Method: Use steaming, grilling, and minimal oil sautéing (opt for coconut oil in moderation).
• Portion Control: Limit carbohydrate servings while prioritizing lean proteins and vegetables.
• Hydration: Drink plenty of water, herbal teas, or lime-infused warm water.

Meal Plan Options

Morning (6:30 AM) - Hydration

  • Option 1: Warm water with lemon and a pinch of turmeric.
  • Option 2: Jeera water or chia seed water.

Breakfast (7:30 - 8:00 AM)

  • Option 1: 1 small Puttu (steamed rice flour and coconut) with green gram curry (moong dal) or kadala curry (black chickpeas, minimal coconut).
  • Option 2: 1-2 Idiyappam (string hoppers) with egg white curry (minimal coconut milk).
  • Option 3: 1-2 small Dosa (fermented rice-lentil pancake) with fish curry.

Add-ons: A slice of papaya or a small piece of nenthrappazham (steamed banana).

Mid-Morning Snack (10:30 AM)

  • Option 1: 1 boiled egg (without yolk) with a sprinkle of pepper and salt.
  • Option 2: A handful of roasted peanuts or chana.

Lunch (1:00 PM)

  • Option 1: 1 small cup of red matta rice, sambar or moru curry (minimal oil), stir-fried or steamed vegetables (beans, cabbage, or thoran with less coconut), Protein source: Grilled or lightly pan-fried fish (e.g., sardines or mackerel).
  • Option 2: Small bowl of kanji (rice porridge) with a side of spicy dry fish chutney (meen curry), steamed vegetables or a light avial (less coconut).
  • Option 3: 1-2 chapati with chicken stew (light, thin coconut milk gravy) and sautéed greens.

Evening Snack (4:00 PM)

  • Option 1: 1 glass of buttermilk with curry leaves and a pinch of salt.
  • Option 2: A small piece of steamed banana or 1 boiled egg.
  • Option 3: A small handful of almonds or cashews (unsalted).

Dinner (7:30 - 8:00 PM)

  • Option 1: Grilled fish with a bowl of clear soup (vegetable or chicken stock-based), A small portion of cucumber-tomato salad with lemon.
  • Option 2: 1 chapati with spicy Kerala-style chicken curry (light gravy, less oil), steamed vegetables or sautéed greens.
  • Option 3: Oats porridge cooked with tempered shallots, curry leaves, and shredded chicken.

Bedtime Drink (Optional)

  • Option 1: A cup of warm turmeric milk (use almond or low-fat milk).
  • Option 2: Warm jeera water.
