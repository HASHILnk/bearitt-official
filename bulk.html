BULKING-DIET

PREMIUM AVAILABLE

A bulking diet is focused on increasing muscle mass and strength, commonly followed by bodybuilders and fitness enthusiasts during a "bulking phase." The key is to create a calorie surplus by consuming more calories than the body requires, while maintaining a high protein intake to support muscle growth and recovery.

Key Principles of a Bulking Diet:

  • Calorie Surplus: Aim for a 10-20% increase in your daily calorie intake compared to your maintenance level
  • Protein:About 1.2–2 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight. This will help in muscle repair and growth.
  • Healthy Fats: Include good fats from sources like nuts, seeds, ghee, and oils to support hormone function and overall health.
  • Hydration: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (at least 3-4 liters), especially around workouts.
  • Carbohydrates: Carbs are your primary energy source, so make sure you’re eating enough to fuel your workouts and recovery.
  • Rest and Recovery: Sleep is crucial for muscle growth—aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep each night.
  • Consistent Training: Follow a structured strength training program, focusing on progressive overload.

SUGGESTED RECIPES

Breakfast

  • Oats (50-100g) cooked with milk (200ml), topped with a banana and peanut butter (1 tbsp).
  • Boiled eggs (4 whole) or scrambled eggs with spinach and onions.
  • Chapathi (3-4 medium) made with whole wheat flour and ghee (1 tsp), served with yogurt (100g) and pomegranate or apple slices.
  • Scrambled eggs (4 whole eggs).
  • Masala chai or black coffee.
  • Grilled chicken breast or fish fillet (seasoned with Indian spices like turmeric, cumin, and coriander).
  • 3-4 roti made with whole wheat flour OR sweet potatoes.

Lunch

  • Grilled chicken breast (200g) or paneer (200g), with brown rice (200g cooked) and mixed vegetable curry (spinach, carrots, beans).
  • Raita (yogurt with cucumber, cumin, and coriander).
  • Tandoori chicken (skinless, marinated in curd and spices, baked or grilled).
  • Sautéed vegetables (spinach, bell peppers, broccoli) with garlic.
  • Brown rice (big portion - 200g).
  • Fish curry or grilled fish (made with minimal oil, with cream) and Indian spices.
  • Brown rice or quinoa (large portion - 200g).

    DINNER

  • Grilled fish or chicken kebabs (200g) with sweet potato (100g) and steamed broccoli or spinach.
  • Cottage cheese (100g) or a small serving of raita.
  • Chicken tikka (grilled 200g) or baked chicken with Indian spices.
  • Vegetable stir fry (use minimal oil, with garlic, cumin, and coriander for flavor).
  • Brown rice (200g).
  • Paneer curry (made with minimal oil).
  • Sautéed spinach or cabbage with mustard seeds and a bit of turmeric.
  • Whole wheat chapathi (1-2).

Snacks

  • Greek yogurt (200g) with mixed nuts (almonds, walnuts) and a handful of raisins or dates.
  • Chana chaat (boiled chickpeas, tomatoes, onions, cucumber, lemon, and spices).
  • A small piece of fruit (mango, papaya, or apple).
  • Protein smoothie with whey protein (1 scoop), banana, oats (30g), and almond milk or regular milk.
  • Boiled eggs (2-3 egg whites) with a sprinkle of salt and pepper.
  • Paneer tikka (grilled, without oil) with a cucumber salad.

    Before Bed

  • Cottage cheese (100g) with a pinch of black pepper or chili powder.
  • A handful of mixed nuts or pumpkin seeds.
  • Greek yogurt (100-150g) with honey (1 tsp) or fruit (berries or apple slices).
  • A boiled egg.
  • Milk (1 glass) with almonds or cashews (5-6 pieces).

ADDITIONAL TIPS

  • Supplements: Consider supplementing with:
    • Whey protein (to meet protein goals)
    • Creatine (to support strength and muscle growth)
    • BCAA (for recovery)
    • Fish oil (for healthy fats and joint support)
  • Caloric Tracking: Track your calories if possible, to ensure you're in a surplus without gaining unnecessary fat.
  • Cooking Methods: Opt for grilling, baking, steaming, and stir-frying with minimal oil.
