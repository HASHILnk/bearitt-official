BULKING-DIET

To build muscle effectively with a bulking diet , it's important to focus on consuming a calorie surplus, while ensuring that the diet is rich in protein, healthy fats, and complex carbohydrates. This helps in muscle recovery and growth without gaining excessive fat.

Key Principles of a Bulking Diet:

  • Calorie Surplus: Aim for a 10-20% increase in your daily calorie intake compared to your maintenance level.
  • Protein: : About 1.6–2.2 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight. This will help in muscle repair and growth
  • Carbohydrates: : Carbs are your primary energy source, so make sure you’re eating enough to fuel your workouts and recovery.( 45-60% of total daily intake)
  • Healthy Fats: Include good fats from sources like nuts, seeds, ghee, and oils to support hormone function and overall health.( : 20-30% of total daily intake)

MAIN PROTEIN SOURCES

  • Paneer (Cottage Cheese): High in protein and healthy fats. Add it to curries, grilled dishes, or salads.
  • Lentils (Dal): Rich in protein and fiber. Have a variety like toor dal, moong dal, or masoor dal daily.
  • Chickpeas (Chana): Include chole or roasted chickpeas as snacks or in curries.
  • Kidney Beans (Rajma): Excellent source of protein, pair with rice for a complete protein meal.
  • Soy Products: Tofu, soy nuggets, and soy milk are excellent protein sources.
  • Quinoa: A complete protein that can be used in place of rice.
  • Greek Yogurt: A rich source of protein, especially for snacks or breakfast.
  • Nuts and Seeds: Almonds, peanuts, chia seeds, and flaxseeds provide good fat and protein.
  • Milk & Dairy Products: Include full-fat milk, curd, and buttermilk for extra calories and protein.

Starches:

Old-fashioned oats • Pre-rinsed quinoa • Brown rice • Sweet potatoes • Russet potatoes • Whole wheat pasta • Ezekiel bread • Farro • Millet • Lentils • Canned chickpeas

Proteins:

Firm tofu • Tempeh • Seitan • Edamame • Canned black beans • Canned kidney beans • Lentils • TVP (textured vegetable protein) • Vegan protein powder • Nutritional yeast • Peanut butter • Almond butter

Fruits and Vegetables:

Navel oranges • Bananas • Fresh baby spinach • Kale • Broccoli • Bell peppers • Avocados • Apples • Zucchini • Yellow squash • Sweet corn • Carrots • Butternut squash • Blueberries • Strawberries • Mushrooms

Other Items:

Chia seeds • Flaxseeds • Hemp seeds • Coconut milk • Almond milk • Cashews • Walnuts • Tahini • Olive oil • Sunflower seeds • Unsweetened cocoa powder • Agave syrup • Dark chocolate (70% or higher)

SUGGESTED RECIPES

Breakfast

  • Oats with Nuts and Fruits
    • Oats cooked in milk, with added almonds, walnuts, and a banana. Add a spoonful of honey or peanut butter for extra calories.
  • Dosa or Idli (4-5) and sambhar with lots of vegetables
    • Masala chai or black coffee.
  • Paneer or Tofu Bhurji with Whole Wheat Toast
    • Scrambled paneer/tofu with onions, tomatoes, and spices. Serve with whole wheat toast for a hearty meal.
  • Chickpea Curry with Chapathi or Puttu
    • Chapathi or puttu (3-4 medium) and ghee (1 tsp), and pomegranate or apple slices.
    • Chickpea curry
    • Masala chai or black coffee.

Lunch

  • Brown Rice with Dal and Vegetables
    • Brown rice or quinoa with dal (lentils), sautéed veggies, and a salad. Add ghee for healthy fats.
  • Rajma Chawal (Kidney Beans with Rice)
    • Rajma with brown or white rice, a salad, and a bowl of curd.
  • Paneer Tikka with Steamed Veggies
    • Serve with roti or brown rice.
  • Sambhar and Brown Rice with Lots of Vegetables
  • Soybean and Vegetable Stir-Fry with Millet
    • Ingredients: Soybeans, bell peppers, broccoli, carrots, millet, and sesame oil.
    • Serve with roti or brown rice.

    DINNER

  • Paneer or Tofu Bhurji with Whole Wheat Toast
    • Scrambled paneer/tofu with onions, tomatoes, and spices. Serve with whole wheat toast for a hearty meal.
  • Chickpea and Quinoa Pulao with Mixed Vegetables
    • Quinoa is cooked with chickpeas, carrots, peas, and bell peppers. Add a spoonful of olive oil or coconut oil for extra calories. This dish provides a balance of carbs, protein, and fats.
  • Stuffed Sweet Potatoes with Lentils
    • Sweet potatoes are baked and filled with spiced lentils. Add some avocado slices or crushed walnuts for extra healthy fats.
  • Mushroom and Spinach Masala with Basmati Rice
    • A rich mushroom and spinach curry with coconut milk, tomatoes, and spices, served with basmati rice. Adding a handful of cashews or almonds can boost the calorie count.

Snacks

  • Greek yogurt (200g) with mixed nuts (almonds, walnuts) and a handful of raisins or dates.
  • Chana chaat (boiled chickpeas, tomatoes, onions, cucumber, lemon, and spices).
    • A small piece of fruit (mango, papaya, or apple).
  • Protein smoothie with whey protein (1 scoop), banana, oats (30g), and almond milk or regular milk.
  • Paneer tikka (grilled, without oil) with a cucumber salad.

    Before Bed

  • Cottage cheese (100g) with a pinch of black pepper or chili powder.
  • A handful of mixed nuts or pumpkin seeds.
  • Greek yogurt (100-150g) with honey (1 tsp) or fruit (berries or apple slices).
  • Milk (1 glass) with almonds or cashews (5-6 pieces).

ADDITIONAL TIPS

  • Spices: Indian spices like turmeric, cumin, coriander, ginger, and garlic are not only flavorful but also help in boosting metabolism.
  • Protein: Aim for 1.6-2g of protein per kg of body weight.
  • Carbs: Prefer complex carbs like oats, quinoa, brown rice, and millets (ragi, bajra).
  • Healthy Fats: Include sources like almonds, walnuts, flax seeds, and olive oil in moderation.
  • Cooking Methods: Opt for grilling, baking, steaming, and stir-frying with minimal oil.
